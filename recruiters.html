Additionally, it's very obvious that you guys have invested close to zero time. Most of the recruiters who contact me have done keyword searches, either on linkedin or google.

For example, I often get job leads in tech stacks I'm uninterested in (say, .net) or industries I won't work in (game development), because I happen to match a search for the right words. Yet a cursory examination of my public repos, or even ten seconds of talking to me, would show that I actively avoid these things.

Ultimately, what I'm trying to say is that you all seem to waste my time, and add little to no value to my career. And so I'm asking nicely: please don't. If you can add value, I'm interested. But if you're just going to take up space in my inbox, please please please don't.


If you're still reading this, and really want to contact me, please read this list of what I want out of my recruiter experience.

  • I will always talk to first party recruiters. If you're an employee of the company you're trying to recruite me to, I will make time for you.
  • Email me, do not phone me. When you phone me, you impose yourself upon me at your own convenience, and at the expense of mine. That's not fair! We haven't even met yet!
  • Respect my timelines. If you see that I just took a new job a month ago, maybe don't try to headhunt me. As an aside, I came to my current job in July 2013
  • I have no hiring authority at my company, and they already have recruiters that they use. Please don't try to place candidates with me. I'm not allowed to accept, so it puts me in a difficult position when you try to sell me on your services.
  • Offer to meet up with me, go out for coffee, drinks, or food. How can you know where I'd be a good match if you don't even know me. And the best way to get to know someone is over tea. Hell, you don't have to pay, just attend. Plus this gives me an easy way to verify that you are actually nearby, and not a guy calling me out of LA while pretending you're here.
  • You have to bring something more to the table than "SOMA startup". There's a ton of those, and I'd get an interview just by walking in their front door.
  • Don't lie to me or disrespect my intelligence. I know how to search the internet for data. Don't tell me that it's unreasonable for me to expect to make 80k as a Rails developer with 3 years of experience.
  • Respect my location. I would like to live and work in SF. Not anywhere else. I don't know why you think I'd take a job in rural Ohio, but I'm not interested, sorry.
  • Above all: Add Value. Many recruiters simply spam me with whatever opportunities they currently have. Please don't do this. I can already go to a job board and look at those if I want to. If you can't definitively say why I would like this job more than another job, AND why this job is more appropriate for me than for someone else, you probably haven't done enough homework.
